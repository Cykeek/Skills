# Persuasion Frameworks for Content & Copywriting

This reference covers the major persuasion frameworks used in content marketing, copywriting, and conversion optimization. Read this when you need to structure persuasive content, choose a framework for a specific goal, or make copy more compelling.

**The principle:** Frameworks aren't templates to fill in. They're structural tools that solve specific reader-psychology problems. Pick the framework that matches the reader's *distance from a decision*, then adapt freely.

---

## 1. Choosing the Right Framework

| When the reader needs... | Use this framework | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| To feel a problem urgently | **PAS** (Problem-Agitate-Solution) | Landing pages, email opens, pain-point content |
| To imagine a better state | **BAB** (Before-After-Bridge) | Case studies, product pages, aspirational content |
| To understand why something works | **FAB** (Features-Advantages-Benefits) | Product docs, feature pages, comparison content |
| To be walked through a decision | **AIDA** (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action) | Long-form sales pages, email sequences |
| To overcome skepticism | **SSA** (Star-Story-Solution) | Case studies, testimonials, founder stories |
| To take a low-friction action | **4Ps** (Picture-Promise-Prove-Push) | Landing pages, CTA optimization |
| To feel compelled by social proof | **Social Proof** (many forms) | Reviews, testimonials, case studies |
| To address the real emotional need | **Storybrand** (7-part framework) | Brand messaging, homepage, about page |

---

## 2. PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)

The most widely used copywriting framework for conversion.

### How it works
| Stage | Job | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Problem** | Name the pain the reader already feels. Be specific: don't be generic. | "Every month, you spend 6 hours manually compiling reports from three different tools." |
| **Agitate** | Make the pain sting. Show the cost of *not* solving it. | "That's 72 hours a year. Hours your team could spend on actual analysis instead of data entry. And every hour spent copying data is an hour you're not catching the trends that matter." |
| **Solution** | Present your product/approach as the natural resolution. | "ReportAI connects to your existing tools and generates a unified dashboard in one click. No more copy-paste. No more manual reconciliation." |

### When to use
- Pain-point-focused content (landing pages, emails, problem-aware audiences)
- Before-PAS: state the reader's current situation
- After-PAS: lead into a case study or offer

### When NOT to use
- The reader doesn't believe they have a problem (use BAB instead)
- The reader is in research mode, not purchase mode (use AIDA or FAB)
- The problem is too trivial to agitate (use FAB or Social Proof)

### PAS variations
- **SAP (Solution-Agitate-Problem)**: Lead with the solution to hook the reader, then agitate to remind them why it matters. Useful for retargeting or warm audiences who already know the problem.
- **PPS (Problem-Preview-Solution)**: Show a glimpse of the solution during the agitate to keep hope alive.

## 3. BAB (Before-After-Bridge)

The most effective framework for aspirational content and transformation stories.

### How it works
| Stage | Job | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Before** | Describe the painful or unsatisfactory current state. Make it recognizable. | "Before Acme, our support team spent 45 minutes per ticket writing responses from scratch." |
| **After** | Describe the transformed future state. Make it vivid and specific. | "Now, they respond in under 5 minutes per ticket. Customer satisfaction is up 30%. The team handles 2x the volume with the same headcount." |
| **Bridge** | Show how your product/service makes the transition possible. | "Acme's AI draft engine learns your team's tone and creates a personalized response in seconds. The agent reviews, tweaks, and hits send." |

### When to use
- Case studies and success stories
- Before/after content (natural fit)
- Aspirational products (fitness, productivity, professional growth)
- Any content where the reader knows the "before" but can't see the "after"

### Why it works
BAB works because humans think in narrative (change over time). By showing the transformation first (before→after), you create tension: *how* did they get there? The bridge resolves that tension.

### BAB tip
Make the After so specific the reader thinks "I want that." Then the Bridge becomes something they *want* to believe in.

## 4. AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)

The classic hierarchy-of-effects framework. Works for longer-form persuasive content.

### How it works
| Stage | Job | Techniques |
|---|---|---|
| **Attention** | Stop the reader mid-scroll. | Bold claim, surprising stat, provocative question, visual hook |
| **Interest** | Keep them reading. Show relevance. | Relatable context, stakes, "this is about you" framing |
| **Desire** | Make them *want* it. | Benefits, social proof, case evidence, scarcity, specificity |
| **Action** | Get the conversion. | Clear CTA, low friction, risk reversal, time boundary (if genuine) |

### Full example
> **A:** "You're losing customers 2 days before they churn."  
> **I:** "I see this with every SaaS at $50-200 MRR. The warning signs are sitting in your data, but most teams don't know what to look for."  
> **D:** "Teams using Predictor see an average 40% reduction in churn within 60 days. Here's how one B2B SaaS cut churn from 7.2% to 4.1% in one quarter."  
> **A:** "Start your free trial. No credit card required. Cancel anytime."

### When to use
- Full landing pages and sales pages
- Email sequences (first email = A, subsequent emails move interest→desire→action)
- Webinar or event promotion

### When NOT to use
- Short social posts (use hook + CTA only)
- Transactional copy where the reader already knows they want to buy
- Internal communications or enablement content

## 5. FAB (Features-Advantages-Benefits)

The logical persuasion framework. Use when the reader is already interested and needs rational reasons.

### How it works
| Layer | Job | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Feature** | What it is. Fact about the product. | "256-bit AES encryption" |
| **Advantage** | What it does. Functional improvement. | "Your data is encrypted both in transit and at rest." |
| **Benefit** | What it *means* to the reader. The emotional/rational payoff. | "So even if there's a breach, your customer data is unreadable. You'll sleep better at night." |

### The FAB test
A good FAB chain passes this test: **"So what?"**
- "256-bit AES encryption." → So what? → "Your data is encrypted in transit and at rest." → So what? → "You're protected from breach liability." → So what? → (if you can't answer, the benefit isn't strong enough)

### When to use
- Feature pages and product comparisons
- Technical audiences who want specs *and* the implications
- Competitive content where you need to show differentiation
- Any rational buyer who needs to justify the decision to others

### FAB + audience
- **Consumer**: Emphasize benefits heavily. Features are evidence.
- **Technical**: Show features, then advantages. Let them infer benefits.
- **Executive**: Show advantages and benefits. Features are optional.

## 6. Storybrand (7-Part Framework)

Donald Miller's brand messaging framework. Use when the customer is the hero, not your brand.

### The 7 parts
1. **A Hero** (the customer, not your brand)
2. **Has a Problem** (external / internal / philosophical)
3. **Meets a Guide** (your brand: not the hero, but the Yoda)
4. **Who Gives Them a Plan** (clear steps)
5. **And Calls Them to Action** (direct, no ambiguity)
6. **That Helps Them Avoid Failure** (the stakes of inaction)
7. **And Ends in Success** (the transformed state)

### When to use
- Homepage and brand messaging
- About page and mission statements
- Any content where the brand needs to position itself as a guide, not a hero

### How to apply to content
Instead of "we built this amazing feature" → "you (the hero) were struggling with X. Here's a simple way to solve it. We can help."

## 7. 4Ps (Picture-Promise-Prove-Push)

A landing-page framework focused on the reader's decision journey.

| Stage | Job |
|---|---|
| **Picture** | Paint a vivid picture of the current pain or the desired future. |
| **Promise** | State what the reader will get. The single clear value proposition. |
| **Prove** | Provide evidence: data, testimonials, case examples, guarantees. |
| **Push** | Ask for the action. Make it clear, specific, and low-friction. |

---

## 8. The 4U Copywriting Formula

Assess and improve your copy by scoring it on 4 dimensions:

| Dimension | What it measures | Low score | High score |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Urgent** | Does it feel timely? | "This is useful eventually." | "You need this now." |
| **Unique** | Does it offer something different? | "We provide quality service." | "We fix the problem your last 3 agencies couldn't." |
| **Useful** | Is the value clear? | Vague benefit | Specific, concrete outcome |
| **Ultra-specific** | Is it precise? | "Improve your workflow." | "Cut reporting time from 6 hours to 30 minutes." |

When feedback says copy is "boring" or "doesn't convert," re-score on these 4 dimensions and fix the lowest one.

## 9. SSA (Star-Story-Solution)

A case-study and testimonial framework that makes the customer the center of attention.

| Stage | Job |
|---|---|
| **Star** | Introduce the customer (or customer profile). Make them relatable. |
| **Story** | Tell what happened: the struggle, the search, the turning point. |
| **Solution** | Show how your product helped. Include specific results. |

The key difference from PAS and BAB: the focus is on a specific person's journey, not a generic problem.

## 10. The 5 Objection-Handling Frameworks

Every persuasive piece should anticipate and answer objections. Here are 5 ways:

| Framework | How it works | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Feel-Felt-Found** | Acknowledge → relate → share solution | "I understand how you feel. Many customers felt the same way. What they found was..." |
| **Pros-Cons-Honest** | List pros, list cons, then weigh them | "Here's what you gain. Here's what you lose. Here's how to decide." |
| **Even-If** | Address the worst case | "Even if you only use this feature, it still pays for itself." |
| **What-This-Isn't** | Define what you're NOT saying | "This isn't about replacing your team. It's about removing their most tedious work." |
| **Liked-Loved-Loathed** | Three-tier testimonial structure | "What our users liked, loved, and loathed. The honest feedback." |

## 11. Adding Persuasion to Any Framework

Regardless of which framework you use, weave in these persuasion principles from Cialdini:

| Principle | What it means | How to use in content |
|---|---|---|
| **Reciprocity** | People give back what they receive | Give valuable free content first (templates, guides) before asking for the sale |
| **Scarcity** | People want what's limited | Genuine time limits, limited spots, exclusive access |
| **Authority** | People follow credible experts | Expert quotes, credentials, certifications, media mentions |
| **Consistency** | People align with past commitments | Remind them of previous actions or stated beliefs |
| **Liking** | People say yes to people they like | Relatable voice, shared values, brand personality |
| **Social Proof** | People follow others' behavior | Testimonials, customer counts, case studies, reviews |
| **Unity** | People trust "us" vs "them" | "We're in this together," community-driven brand, shared identity |

## 12. Framework Selection Quick Reference

| Goal | Primary Framework | Secondary to layer |
|---|---|---|
| Get someone to try a free trial | PAS or 4Ps | Social proof, FAB for features |
| Sell to skeptical buyers | BAB + SSA | Objection handling, authority |
| Present a case study | BAB or SSA | Social proof, FAB |
| Write a homepage | Storybrand or 4Ps | Social proof, BAB for transformation |
| Write a product/feature page | FAB | PAS for pain points, SSA for proof |
| Write an email sequence | AIDA (per email) | PAS (opens), BAB (stories) |
| Launch a new product | BAB | AIDA (full page), social proof |
| Write comparison content | FAB + honest pros/cons | Objection handling |
| Write thought leadership | 4U or contrast framework | Authority, consistency |
| Overcome price objections | Value reframe + FAB | Objection handling, social proof |
