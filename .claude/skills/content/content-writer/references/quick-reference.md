# Content Writer Quick Reference

One-page cheatsheet for fast content decisions. Use the deep reference files when you need nuance.

## 1. Framework Selection (persuasion-frameworks.md §12)

| Goal | Primary Framework | Layer With |
|---|---|---|
| Free trial signup | PAS or 4Ps | Social proof, FAB |
| Skeptical buyers | BAB + SSA | Objection handling, authority |
| Case study | BAB or SSA | Social proof, FAB |
| Homepage | Storybrand or 4Ps | Social proof, BAB |
| Product/feature page | FAB | PAS for pain, SSA for proof |
| Email sequence | AIDA per email | PAS for opens, BAB for stories |
| Product launch | BAB | AIDA, social proof |
| Comparison content | FAB + honest pros/cons | Objection handling |
| Thought leadership | 4U or contrast | Authority, consistency |
| Price objections | Value reframe + FAB | Objection handling, social proof |

## 2. Tone Dials By Audience (style-guide.md §5)

| Audience | Tone | Do | Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| Developers | Precise, technical, peer-to-peer | Show code, name APIs, admit trade-offs | Marketing language, hand-waving |
| Executives | Crisp, results-first, scannable | Lead with outcome, quantify impact, get to ask | Process minutiae, jargon walls, hedging |
| Consumers | Relatable, warm, benefits-first | Use "you," micro-stories, busy-life context | Spec sheets, corporate voice |
| First-time visitors | Orienting, reassuring | Define terms, point to start-here path | Inside baseball, unexplained acronyms |
| PMs/analysts/designers | Bridging, clear, useful | Explain technical choices through user/business impact | "Magic" explanations or stack traces |
| Stressed readers | Calm, brief, action-first | Acknowledge friction, give fix, offer next step | Cheerfulness, long preambles |

## 3. Headline Formulas (content-frameworks.md §5)

| Formula | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Numbered utility | `[Number] [adjective] [things] for [audience]` | "11 underrated tools for solo founders" |
| Pain removal | `How to [do thing] without [pain]` | "How to ship a redesign without losing SEO traffic" |
| Counterintuitive why | `Why [counterintuitive claim]` | "Why your best customers are the ones who complain" |
| Guide | `The [adjective] guide to [topic] (year)` | "The no-bullshit guide to webhooks (2025)" |
| Unexpected comparison | `[Familiar thing] is [unexpected comparison]` | "Pricing pages are salespeople in disguise" |
| Authority outcome | `[Authority] [secret verb] [outcome]` | "How Linear shipped 100 features without burning out" |
| Topic promise | `[Topic]: [specific promise]` | "Internal docs: how to make people actually read them" |

**Headline test:** specific promise, concrete nouns/verbs, curiosity without clickbait, delivers on the article.

## 4. Em-Dash Replacement Rules (style-guide.md §2)

| Replace em dash with | Use when |
|---|---|
| Period | The break is a full stop or second complete thought |
| Comma | The aside is light or non-essential |
| Colon | Introducing an explanation, consequence, or list |
| Parentheses | Adding a clarifying aside |
| Rephrase | The sentence is trying to do too much |

**Rule:** em dashes are banned in body prose unless reproducing a verbatim quote, technical syntax, or explicit user instruction.

## 5. Card/Grid Parity Rules (content-frameworks.md §12)

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| Default card shape | Short heading + 1-2 sentence body |
| Use bullets only for | Checklists, specs, distinct steps, discrete comparisons |
| Heading length | 2-5 words; same grid within 2 words of each other |
| Body length | 15-35 words; same row within 10 words |
| 3-column grid | Target 20-25 body words per card |
| 4-column grid | Target 12-18 body words per card |
| 2-column grid | Up to 35-45 words, still within 10 words of pair |
| Final pass | Count words. If row range exceeds 15 words, revise. |

## 6. Banned Opening Patterns (editing-checklist.md)

| Banned opener | Quick fix |
|---|---|
| "In today's [X] landscape..." | Start with the reader's specific pressure point |
| "In this article, I will..." | Cut it and start with the first real idea |
| "It is important to understand that..." | State the important thing directly |
| "As we all know..." | Skip it if known; teach it if not |
| "[Topic] is a critical component of modern [field]." | Use a specific observation or reader question |
| "In recent years, [trends]..." | Name the specific trend and consequence |
| "Let's talk about X" | Make a claim or ask a sharper question |
| "I'm excited to share..." | Center the reader, not the writer |

**Better openings:** reader situation, expectation reversal, direct thesis, reader question, specific scene.

## 7. Email Subject Line Formulas (templates.md §3)

| Formula | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Direct benefit | `[Outcome] in [timeframe]` | "Your churn report in 5 minutes" |
| Curiosity gap | `Why [specific thing] [unexpected outcome]` | "Why your welcome email is killing signups" |
| Number | `[Number] [things] for [audience/goal]` | "3 fixes for your pricing page" |
| Shipped update | `[Feature/update]: [concrete benefit]` | "New: bulk-edit saves 40 minutes a week" |
| Honest admission | `We [honest thing]. Here's what we learned.` | "We killed our most-loved feature. Here's what replaced it." |
| Reader problem | `[Their pain], fixed` or `Still [struggling with X]?` | "Still exporting reports manually?" |
| Conversational | `[First-person, low-key, real]` | "Quick question about your dashboard" |
| Contrast | `[Old way] vs [new way]` | "Manual reviews vs automated: the real tradeoff" |
| Tease | `[Hint at value without giving it away]` | "The pricing trick almost no one uses" |
| Scarcity | `[Deadline] + [specific stakes]` | "Seats close Friday: last chance" |

**Subject test:** under 50 chars, specific, paid off in first sentence, not deceptive.

## 8. Content Performance Benchmarks (SKILL.md §8)

| Metric | Weak | Average | Strong |
|---|---|---|---|
| Blog organic CTR | < 1% | 2-4% | > 5% |
| Email open rate, cold | < 20% | 25-35% | > 40% |
| Email open rate, warm | < 30% | 35-50% | > 55% |
| Email CTR | < 1% | 2-4% | > 5% |
| Landing page CVR, cold | < 1% | 2-5% | > 8% |
| LinkedIn engagement | < 1% | 2-4% | > 5% |
| Twitter/X engagement | < 0.5% | 1-3% | > 4% |

| Content type | Primary signal | Warning sign |
|---|---|---|
| Blog post | Organic traffic, 3-6 month lag | High bounce + low scroll = wrong audience or weak hook |
| Landing page | CVR | CVR < 1% cold = trust, copy, or offer problem |
| Marketing email | CTR | High open + low CTR = weak body or CTA mismatch |
| Case study | Sales cycle influence | Sales rarely shares it = not in customer language |
| Social post | Engagement rate | Likes without shares/saves = entertaining but not useful |
| Comparison page | Mid-funnel conversion | Exits to competitor = losing the argument |
| Whitepaper | Lead quality | Downloads but no pipeline = wrong audience or gated too early |

## 9. The 4 Clarifying Questions (SKILL.md §3)

Ask only what is missing:

1. **Audience:** Who is reading? Role, knowledge level, cares, prior context.
2. **Goal:** What should this piece do? Educate, persuade, inform, entertain, convert, nurture, reassure, warn.
3. **Format & length:** Where will it live? Blog, email, landing page, social, PDF, print. How long?
4. **Tone & voice:** What should the reader feel? Confident, reassured, curious, urgent, inspired. Any brand examples?

**If user gives 3 of 4:** infer the fourth and state the assumption.

## 10. Final 30-Second Scan

- Framework fits reader's decision stage.
- Tone matches audience, not generic "professional."
- Opening avoids banned patterns.
- Headline promises something specific.
- No em dashes in body prose.
- Cards use heading + prose unless they are specs, steps, or checklists.
- Grid rows pass heading/body word-count parity.
- Email subject is short, specific, and paid off immediately.
- Benchmarks are treated as rough guides, not absolutes.
