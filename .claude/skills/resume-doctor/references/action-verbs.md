# Action Verbs — Deep Reference

This is the deep reference for selecting high-impact action verbs. Bullet points in the experience section should always begin with an active action verb to convey agency, leadership, and concrete accomplishments.

---

## 1. Leadership, Management, & Collaboration

Use these to highlight ownership, direction, team leadership, project coordination, and cross-functional work.

| Strong Action Verbs | Verbs to Avoid (Weak) |
|---|---|
| Led, Spearheaded, Directed, Orchestrated, Guided, Managed, Chaired, Governed, Managed, Commanded, Coordinated, Governed, Mentored, Trained, Steered, Facilitated, Mobilized, Synchronized | Was in charge of, Helped with, Worked on, Worked with, Served as, Coordinated with |

### Examples
- **Spearheaded** an agile transformation program for 4 teams, increasing delivery velocity by 18%.
- **Orchestrated** task shifting and sprint planning for 12 engineers across 2 time zones.
- **Mentored** 5 junior developers, helping 3 achieve promotions within 12 months.

---

## 2. Technical, Development, & Engineering

Use these to highlight technical execution, system creation, architecture, coding, database management, and automation.

| Strong Action Verbs | Verbs to Avoid (Weak) |
|---|---|
| Engineered, Built, Coded, Programmed, Architected, Designed, Automated, Refactored, Developed, Deployed, Debugged, Scripted, Implemented, Integrated, Modernized, Provisioned | Managed, Programmed in, Coding, Had to write, Utilized |

### Examples
- **Architected** a cloud migration plan moving legacy databases to AWS Aurora, saving $14,000 monthly.
- **Automated** system health checks targeting container logs to reduce server outages by 35%.
- **Refactored** 12 outdated legacy API endpoints, reducing median response latency by 120ms.

---

## 3. Operations, Efficiency, & Process Improvement

Use these to highlight optimization, process mapping, overhead reduction, logistics, and organizational design.

| Strong Action Verbs | Verbs to Avoid (Weak) |
|---|---|
| Streamlined, Optimized, Restructured, Overhauled, Simplified, Condensed, Maximized, Standardized, Centralized, Systematized, Automated, Revitalized | Maintained, Worked on, Handled, Assisted in |

### Examples
- **Streamlined** the customer support ticketing flow to reduce response times from 3 hours to 45 minutes.
- **Overhauled** the vendor intake onboarding pipeline, reducing setup delays by 8 days.
- **Standardized** development environments across 4 teams, cutting onboarding time for new hires by 50%.

---

## 4. Creative, Design, & Research

Use these to highlight ideation, UI/UX conceptualization, client research, data collection, visual design, and content strategy.

| Strong Action Verbs | Verbs to Avoid (Weak) |
|---|---|
| Designed, Formulated, Authored, Conceptualized, Illustrated, Researched, Analyzed, Audited, Prototyped, Conducted, Interviewed, Translated, Created, Modeled | Drew up, Did design, Wrote out, Prepared, Helped create |

### Examples
- **Conceptualized** and designed a new workspace UI layout, yielding a 19% increase in click-through rates.
- **Designed** a visual component system library used by 3 client applications.
- **Audited** user behavior paths using click maps to locate and fix a checkout conversion drop.

---

## 5. Sales, Business Development, & Marketing

Use these to highlight growth, client acquisition, revenue production, lead generation, branding, and closing deals.

| Strong Action Verbs | Verbs to Avoid (Weak) |
|---|---|
| Generated, Expanded, Converted, Negotiated, Secured, Captured, Accelerated, Penetrated, Cultivated, Launched, Positioned, Pitching | Sold, Found, Made calls, Dealt with, Worked on |

### Examples
- **Generated** $1.2M in annual recurring revenue (ARR) from 14 targeted Enterprise clients.
- **Accelerated** outbound pipeline velocity by 40% through targeted outreach.
- **Secured** a key partnership agreement with a national distributor, extending local market coverage.

---

## 6. Financial, Analysis, & Data

Use these to highlight quantitative review, metric audits, forecasting, cost structures, and data science modeling.

| Strong Action Verbs | Verbs to Avoid (Weak) |
|---|---|
| Analyzed, Audited, Forecasted, Quantified, Modeled, Extrapolated, Calculated, Visualized, Formed, Managed, Conserved, Allocated | Checked, Counted, Summarized, Evaluated, Looked after |

### Examples
- **Forecasted** cash requirements accurately within 4% of actuals, optimizing working capital reserves.
- **Audited** internal expense reports, identifying redundancies that saved $45,000 annually.
- **Visualized** sales data patterns for executive decision support using Tableau dashboards.

---

## 7. Eliminating Weak/Passive Helper Phrases

Never start experience bullets with helper, passive, or vague verbs. Replace them using this rewrite sheet:

| Avoid Helper Phrase | Strong Replacement (Action Verb) |
|---|---|
| *Responsible for managing customer files* | **Maintained** customer records or **Optimized** client database efficiency... |
| *Worked with developers to design dashboards* | **Coordinated** with developers to launch dashboards or **Collaborated** on dashboard layouts... |
| *Duties included writing tests* | **Engineered** test suites or **Authored** test cases... |
| *Helped create the onboarding process* | **Co-authored** or **Redesigned** onboarding processes... |
| *Assisted in preparing monthly budgets* | **Formulated** or **Drafted** monthly budgets... |
| *Served as a points-contact for errors* | **Resolved** ticket errors or **Managed** resolution pathways... |
